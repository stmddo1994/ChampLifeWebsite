Why ChampLife?

As a professional athlete, I've been very fortunate to meet people from “all walks of life”. Titans of industry to the unemployed, the wealthy to the poor, inner city to the suburbs... People of all races… The young and the old… No matter the background, many of these people had common adversity by either suffering from these conditions personally or having a person in their life dealing with anxiety, depression, addiction, alcoholism, low self-esteem, weight, and health issues. Dealing with the frustrations of day-to-day life with nowhere or no one to turn. I found myself in this position and after overcoming addiction, depression, and anxiety ChampLife was birthed.

As a non-profit, ChampLife provides mentoring to at-risk teens as well as adults, through counseling, life coaching, and boxing fitness. We are dedicated to academic, and personal growth and development, and teaching people how to navigate and overcome day-to-day challenges and life adversities.

Ken Lunkins, President/CEO

Statement of Need

Violence is an important public health problem among adolescents in the United States. Demographic factors include poverty, ethnic-minority group membership, gender (male), and age. Additional factors include difficulty coping with anger and frustration, low self-efficacy, poor problem-solving, and poor social skills (Botvin, Griffin, & Nichols, 2006) (Flay, Gramlich, Segawa, Burns, & Holliday, 2004).

Furthermore, Florida’s high rate of incarceration will have tremendous social costs to its residents. As of 2015, there were 100,050 inmates in Florida prisons (Department of Corrections, State of Florida). The incarceration of a family member hurts children’s prospects of educational success. Children of incarcerated parents are at risk for developing behavior (Murray & Farrington, 2005) and school problems (Arditti, Lambert-Shute, & Joest, 2003), and insecure attachment relationships (Poehlmann, 2005b). Parental imprisonment appears to affect children over and above separation experiences and associated risks and predicted boys’ internalizing problems from age 14 to 48 (Murray J, Farrington DP., 2005). These children are a vulnerable group who experience multiple risks, making them an ideal candidate for interventions. In Broward County, FL, alone, during the year 2014-2015, 129 children were removed from their households to foster care due to a parent’s incarceration. Statistically, 37% of Broward’s children reside in single-parent households, 23 % have inadequate social support, and 46% of children are eligible for free lunch. During the 2013-2014 school years, Broward County Public Schools reported a total of 100,171 suspensions, of which over 50% were due to disruptive behaviors. These statistics clearly indicate that Broward County’s youth need the opportunity to develop secure attachments through mentoring.

Objective

At its core, youth mentoring is designed to be a corrective experience for at-risk youth who have experienced poor relations with parents or other caregivers (Grossman, Chan, Schwartz, & Rhodes, 2012). Defined more broadly, mentoring programs are designed to improve child well-being in terms of three overarching domains: cognitive development, health and safety, and social and emotional stability (Herrera, DuBois, & Grossman, 2013; Jekielek, Moore, & Hair, 2002). Attachment theory, as coined by John Bowlby, established the precedent that childhood development depended heavily upon a child's ability to form a strong relationship with "at least one primary caregiver" and concluded that a strong attachment to a caregiver provides a necessary sense of security and foundation (Bowlby, John. Attachment and Loss. 1969).

To address the three domains, ChampLife, a 501© 3 non-profit organization, will offer the opportunity for youth to establish strong relationships through its boxing fitness and counseling program. Youth will be offered the opportunity to participate in after-school tutoring sessions, life skills coaching, boxing fitness, and cognitive behavioral counseling to affect change in their academic skills, physical fitness, and thinking errors.

ChampLife aims to provide mentoring relationships to a total of thirty (30) young people per year during two (2) ninety (90) day sessions and one (1) summer sessions. The target population for the program will be at-risk/high-risk youth, ages 16 to 24, who have been chronically suspended, expelled, juvenile justice-involved, and/or are children of incarcerated parents. Four (4) adult staff members and screened assistant volunteer trainers will mentor the young people to establish a mentoring bond and to ensure that a mentor is always available to the young person. Mentoring will be provided at the ChampLife location, allowing the program to supervise mentors and mentees.

ChampLife will focus on the following outcomes:

  1. Identify three personal and fitness goals and achieve two of the three goals during the mentoring period.
  2. Identify and achieve one community service goal.
  3. Improve life and social skills, measured by a survey instrument.
  4. Improve or maintain school performance, verified by the mentee's school.

Evidence-based programs and practices, such as cognitive behavioral therapy, motivational interviewing, WRAP groups, and interactive journaling, will guide and motivate mentees through the process of behavioral change.

ChampLife will offer case management, counseling, and support groups to the mentees' families as wraparound services to support their success through its affiliations with Trees of Hope, Celebrate Recovery, and AA/NA.

ChampLife will collaborate with other outside agencies, such as the OIC of South Florida, Children’s Services Counsel, Smith Community Mental Health Family Strengthening program, Broward Public Schools, the Department of Juvenile Justice, and the Florida Department of Children and Families, through which referrals may be made. Community partnerships will be developed with other organizations to help support mentoring relationships. ChampLife's goal is to work in partnership with the community to promote program effectiveness.

Counseling

We will offer counseling to individuals one-on-one, to couples and families, as well as offer multiple support groups. We will have licensed and lay counselors who handle marital, family, parental, and career issues, as well as depression, anxiety, trauma, grief, divorce, compulsive addictive behaviors, and more.

We hope to provide Counseling services for such needs as:

  • Parenting, Parent-Child Relationships
  • Child and Adolescent Counseling
  • Marital and Family Dysfunction
  • Depression and Anxiety
  • Sexual Abuse
  • Traumatic Experiences
  • Grief and Loss Recovery
  • ACOA and Codependency
  • Compulsive and Addictive Behaviors
  • Chemical Dependency

Counseling: Counseling is a service in which an individual seeks guidance to help them resolve life issues, handle a crisis, and/or motivate them towards change. Through each session, we seek to help those in need attain healing and peace from their current struggles in a loving and confidential environment.

Counseling benefits to individuals:

  • Develop new perspectives and life skills so that you can free yourself from habitual patterns of anxiety, depression, and self-criticism.
  • Deal with addictive behaviors including substance abuse, relationship issues, gambling eating disorders, etc.
  • Reclaim your sense of personal power. Learn to take responsibility and move forward in your life rather than feeling stuck, helpless, and hopeless.
  • Find ways to relate to emotional or physical suffering so that inevitable suffering brings wisdom & compassion rather than depression & anger.
  • Improve the quality of your relationships by developing more life-affirming behaviors.
  • Provide you with a set scheduled time to attend to your own needs and regain your perspective.

The Benefits of Physical Activity:

Regular physical activity is one of the most important things you can do for your health. It can help:

  • Control your weight
  • Reduce your risk of cardiovascular disease
  • Reduce your risk for type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome
  • Reduce your risk of some cancers
  • Strengthen your bones and muscles
  • Improve your mental health and mood
  • Improve your ability to do daily activities and prevent falls, if you're an older adult
  • Increase your chances of living longer

The Benefits of AA/NA Meetings: The primary benefit of attending an AA meeting is to interact with, learn from, and engage in mutual support with others who are also struggling with alcoholism.

  • AA/NA meetings provide individuals with an ongoing source of support at all stages of the recovery process.
  • AA/NA meetings also give people in long-term recovery the opportunity to mentor and otherwise assist others who are new to the AA/NA meeting concept.
  • The AA/NA program operates when a recovered alcoholic passes along the story of his or her own problem drinking, describes the sobriety he or she has found in AA, and invites the newcomer to join the informal Fellowship.

Life Skill Education and Tutoring

Life Skills Education has long-term benefits to the society. These include educational, social, health, cultural, and economic benefits.

(a) Educational benefits

  • Leads to desirable behavior change.
  • Improves discipline in schools.
  • Reduces learner problems such as truancy, absenteeism, drug and substance abuse, and teenage pregnancies.
  • Helps learners to improve their performance.

(b) Social benefits

  • Enables learners to choose good and reliable friends.
  • Helps learners to use their leisure time properly.
  • Assists learners to recognize and avoid risky situations.
  • Helps in character building.

(c) Health benefits

  • Leads to prevention and control of diseases such as STIs, HIV, and AIDS.
  • Contributes to a person’s general well-being (physical, mental, emotional, and social).
  • Leads to less strain on health facilities.
  • Helps people to be responsible for their own and other people’s health.

(d) Cultural benefits

  • Enables people to adopt and maintain meaningful cultural practices and avoid practices that may put themselves and others at risk.
  • Promotes harmonious interaction between people of different cultures.
  • Helps in the clarification of values in society.

(e) Economic benefits

  • It leads to high productivity due to a motivated, strong, and energetic labor force.
  • Resources such as time and money are saved as learners acquire skills to manage themselves and their environment.
  • Rehabilitation of drug and substance abuse and repair of damaged property.

The tutoring program has two main benefits:

  • To allow inner-city students to receive tutoring services that they would not normally be able to afford.
  • To provide outstanding students with an opportunity to earn income for their post-secondary education.

Currently Planned Activities:

Champ Life Ministries will offer counseling to the public on a bi-weekly basis. Currently, the total time that counseling will be offered is 16-20 hours a week. Counseling will consist of one (1) hour of scheduled appointments. Six (6) hours would be set aside for walk-in and crises.

Champ Life will co-labor with several community-based non-profit organizations and AA and NA to provide support groups. Groups will meet weekly and ongoing. The total time allocation for these groups will be 10 hours a week with counselors yet to be assigned. The breakdown for types of groups: AA (2 hours per day, 3 times a week); NA (2 hours per day, 3 times a week); Celebrate Recovery (once a week for an hour); Divorce Care/Grief Share (one a week for 1.5 hours); Trees of Hope (once a week for 13 weeks for 2 hours – 3 times a year).

Life skills coaching and after-school tutoring will be available Monday through Friday in the afternoons. We plan to purchase a van for transportation to bring students to the facility from local Middle and High Schools. We will solicit, background check, and screen volunteers to run this program. Other activities and programs will be added as needed and supported by the community.

Benefits to Individuals

Champ Life will provide an opportunity for individuals to confidentially make changes in their relationships, their finances, and their health to become healthy and productive people in society. Champ Life would like to establish after-school tutoring to help at-risk teens stay in school and further their education; provide opportunities to stop prison recidivism through education, support groups and job training; and offer life skills classes weekly to help people function in society. Champ Life’s physical fitness therapy will help lower the risk of disease, further medical treatment, and establish a healthy outlet for those struggling with anger, depression, and anxiety.
